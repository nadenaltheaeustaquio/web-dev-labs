Common Mistakes

Every beginner makes mistakes, and that is part of the learning process. However, some common errors can be avoided with awareness. Overwatering is one of the most frequent mistakes, often made by beginners who think more water means healthier plants. Another mistake is planting too many crops at once, which becomes overwhelming to maintain. Beginners may also forget to check sunlight needs, resulting in weak or leggy plants. Using poor soil without adding nutrients is another problem that affects growth. Some new gardeners also place pots without drainage, causing roots to rot. The best way to avoid these mistakes is to start small, observe your plants, and adjust based on their needs. Gardening is about progress, not perfection.
